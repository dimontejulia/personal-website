About Me

Education


I started attending Queen's University in the September of 2015 after graduating from St. Clement's School in Toronto. I have loved my time in Kingston, Ontario for the past 3 years learning from inspiring professors, getting involved in campus life and making lifelong memories with friends.

I am currently working on my Bachelor of Computing with Honours in Computer Science and am set to graduate in the spring of 2019.

Extracurriculars

I am very involved with campus activities at Queen's. I have worked at the campus coffee shop, Common Ground, as a cashier for the past two years and working with, and for other students. This past year, I was president for the Women in Computing organization helping to plan socials, organize outreach and informational events as well as promote the importance of women in technology.

Marketing has always been something I am interested in, serving as the marketing co-director for Soul Food (an organization that takes the leftover food from cafeterias and delivers it to shelters near by.) This upcoming year I am also the marketing director for QHacks, the second largest committe on campus for Queen's annual Hackathon.

Hobbies

Outside of school and campus life, I love to be active, especially by playing sports. Since first year, I have been competing on the Queen's tennis team. Additionally, I have participated on Queen's intramural sports teams such as volleyball and dodgeball.

Being on exchange this semester, travelling has been one of my favourite hobbies. Visiting new countries, meeting new people and learning new cultures has been an amazing experience.
